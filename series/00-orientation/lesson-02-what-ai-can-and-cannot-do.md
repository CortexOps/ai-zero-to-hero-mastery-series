# Lesson 02: What AI Can and Cannot Do

## Skill Level

Beginner

## Time Needed

15–20 minutes

---

# Who This Helps

This lesson is for anyone beginning to use AI who wants realistic expectations.

---

# What You Will Learn

By the end of this lesson, you will:

* Understand AI strengths
* Understand AI weaknesses
* Learn why AI sometimes fails
* Build safer expectations
* Improve critical thinking

---

# Why This Matters

Many people either:

* Overestimate AI
  or
* Underestimate AI

Both mistakes create problems.

Understanding AI realistically helps you:

* Use it better
* Avoid errors
* Stay safe
* Save time
* Improve results

---

# Learn

AI is powerful at:

* Writing
* Summarizing
* Brainstorming
* Organizing
* Explaining
* Pattern recognition
* Productivity support

---

# AI Is NOT Perfect At:

* Always being accurate
* Understanding nuance perfectly
* Replacing experts
* Legal decisions
* Medical certainty
* Financial certainty
* Ethical judgment
* Real-time truth without verification

---

# AI Can:

## Examples:

* Rewrite emails
* Create checklists
* Explain concepts
* Draft reports
* Suggest workflows
* Summarize documents
* Help brainstorm ideas

---

# AI Cannot Reliably:

## Examples:

* Guarantee truth
* Replace human accountability
* Know confidential context automatically
* Provide perfect legal advice
* Provide perfect medical advice
* Make final business decisions alone

---

# See

### Good Use:

```text id="s94kzb"
Summarize this workplace safety policy in simple terms.
```

### Risky Use:

```text id="w3kcnf"
Should I ignore this chest pain?
```

---

# Do

Ask AI:

```text id="g0ojx7"
Give me five examples of tasks AI can help with in my job, and three examples where human expertise is still essential.
```

---

# Reflect

Ask yourself:

* Where could AI save me time?
* Where should I verify carefully?
* Where should humans remain fully responsible?

---

# Critical Rule

> AI should assist decisions, not blindly make them.

---

# Common Beginner Mistake

Mistake:

> “AI said it, so it must be true.”

Better mindset:

> “AI gave me a starting point. I must verify.”

---

# Safety Check

Always verify when using AI for:

* Health
* Finance
* Legal
* Employment
* Security
* Public communication

---

# Real-World Challenge

Use AI for one safe practical task today, such as:

* Writing
* Planning
* Learning
* Summarizing

Then evaluate:

* Accuracy
* Helpfulness
* Time saved

---

# Key Takeaway

> AI is a powerful assistant, not an infallible authority.

---

# Final Advice

The best AI users understand both:

* Capability
* Limitation

This balance creates safer, smarter outcomes.
