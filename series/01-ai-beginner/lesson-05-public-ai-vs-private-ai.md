# Lesson 05: Public AI vs Private AI

## Skill Level

Beginner

## Time Needed

15–20 minutes

---

# Who This Helps

Anyone using AI for:

* Work
* School
* Business
* Government
* Healthcare
* IT
* Personal productivity

---

# What You Will Learn

By the end of this lesson, you will:

* Understand public AI tools
* Understand private/local AI tools
* Learn data privacy risks
* Know when public AI is safe
* Know when private AI is safer
* Build professional AI habits

---

# Why This Matters

Many people unknowingly share sensitive information with public AI tools.

This can create risks involving:

* Personal privacy
* Business confidentiality
* Student data
* Medical data
* Financial records
* Security information

---

# Public AI Defined

Public AI tools include:

* ChatGPT web
* Claude web
* Gemini web
* Copilot cloud tools
* Other online AI services

---

# Benefits of Public AI

## Advantages:

* Easy to use
* Fast
* Often powerful
* Accessible anywhere
* Low cost or free

---

# Risks of Public AI

## Potential concerns:

* Sensitive data exposure
* Policy violations
* Compliance issues
* Cloud storage concerns
* Third-party processing

---

# Important Rule

> Never paste confidential, protected, or regulated information into public AI unless approved.

---

# Private AI Defined

Private AI includes:

* Local LLMs
* Offline AI
* Self-hosted AI
* Enterprise-controlled AI systems

Examples:

* Ollama
* LM Studio
* Local GPT
* Secure enterprise deployments

---

# Benefits of Private AI

## Advantages:

* Better privacy
* More control
* Internal security
* Regulatory compliance
* Customization
* Reduced external risk

---

# Limitations of Private AI

## Challenges:

* Hardware requirements
* Setup complexity
* Maintenance
* Possible lower model performance
* Technical knowledge

---

# Simple Comparison

## Public AI:

* Easy
* Fast
* Convenient
* Higher privacy risk

## Private AI:

* Safer for sensitive work
* More complex
* More secure
* More customizable

---

# Example Safe Public AI Uses

* Resume help
* Email drafting
* Brainstorming
* Learning
* Personal planning
* Marketing ideas

---

# Example Private AI Preferred Uses

* Student records
* Medical notes
* HR files
* Legal records
* Security logs
* Internal company strategy
* Confidential reports

---

# Real-World Professional Rule

### If data is sensitive:

Use private or approved enterprise AI.

---

# See

### Unsafe:

```text id="cf0kp7"
Here are student names, records, and discipline reports...
```

### Safer:

```text id="b7pt6y"
How can I improve student support communication generally?
```

---

# Beginner Security Checklist

Before using AI, ask:

* Is this confidential?
* Is this regulated?
* Is this personal?
* Is this business-sensitive?
* Is this legally protected?

If YES:
Use caution or private AI.

---

# Public AI Best Practices

* Remove names
* Remove private identifiers
* Generalize data
* Follow company policies
* Verify outputs
* Understand terms of service

---

# Growing Trend

Organizations increasingly deploy private AI because of:

* Security
* Compliance
* Data ownership
* Long-term cost savings
* Operational trust

---

# Reflection

Ask:

* Would I share this publicly?
* Could this violate policy?
* Is local AI a better option?
* Am I protecting others’ data?

---

# Common Beginner Mistakes

* Sharing sensitive work data
* Using AI without policy review
* Ignoring compliance
* Assuming all AI tools are equally private
* Prioritizing convenience over security

---

# Key Takeaway

> Public AI is powerful, but privacy awareness is essential.

---

# Final Advice

Use:

## Public AI for:

* General productivity
* Learning
* Drafting
* Personal use

## Private AI for:

* Sensitive work
* Regulated environments
* Professional security
* Confidential operations

---

# Real-World Challenge

Review one AI task you perform.

Ask:

* Could this expose sensitive data?
* Should this be done privately?

---

# Confidence Builder

Understanding privacy makes you:

* More professional
* More secure
* More trustworthy
* Better prepared for future AI systems

---

# Final Rule

> Smart AI users protect data as carefully as they use technology.
