# Lesson 04: Why AI Sometimes Gets Things Wrong

## Skill Level

Beginner

## Time Needed

15–20 minutes

---

# Who This Helps

This lesson is for anyone using AI who wants accurate, trustworthy, and safe results.

---

# What You Will Learn

By the end of this lesson, you will:

* Understand why AI can produce incorrect answers
* Learn what hallucinations are
* Recognize common AI mistakes
* Build safer verification habits
* Improve your judgment

---

# Why This Matters

AI can sound:

* Smart
* Professional
* Confident
* Detailed

And still be wrong.

This is one of the most important truths for every AI user.

---

# Learn

## AI does NOT truly “know” facts like humans do.

AI predicts likely next words based on patterns.

That means it can:

* Misunderstand
* Invent information
* Misquote
* Use outdated info
* Overgeneralize
* Sound certain when uncertain

---

# Common Reasons AI Gets Things Wrong

## 1. Missing context

Bad prompts produce weaker answers.

## 2. Limited data

AI may lack updated information.

## 3. Pattern prediction

AI generates likely responses, not guaranteed truth.

## 4. Hallucination

AI may fabricate sources, facts, or details.

---

# What Is a Hallucination?

A hallucination is when AI generates false or misleading information as though it were true.

---

# Example

### User:

```text id="u0a7pw"
Give me a source for this law.
```

### AI:

May provide:

* Fake legal citations
* Incorrect websites
* Nonexistent references

---

# Important Rule

> AI output should be reviewed, not blindly trusted.

---

# Real-World Risk Examples

## Dangerous Uses:

* Medical advice
* Legal advice
* Financial decisions
* Security decisions
* Employment policies
* Compliance decisions

---

# Safer Uses:

* Drafting
* Brainstorming
* Summarizing
* Rewriting
* Planning
* Learning basics

---

# See

### Unsafe Thinking:

```text id="9an7kc"
AI said it, so it must be true.
```

### Safe Thinking:

```text id="5ytmqp"
AI gave me a starting point, now I verify it.
```

---

# Verification Checklist

Before trusting AI:

* Check facts
* Confirm sources
* Verify dates
* Cross-check important details
* Use human judgment
* Ask follow-up questions

---

# Beginner Safety Formula

## Use AI for:

* Assistance
* Drafting
* Learning
* Productivity

## Do NOT rely on AI alone for:

* Critical life decisions
* Sensitive legal matters
* Security response
* Medical emergencies

---

# Do

Take one AI answer and ask:

```text id="j3y4zd"
How confident are you in this answer?
What sources support this?
What could be incorrect?
```

---

# Reflection

Ask:

* Did AI sound more certain than it should?
* Was verification needed?
* How could mistakes cause problems?
* How can I protect myself?

---

# Common Beginner Mistakes

* Blind trust
* No verification
* Sharing sensitive data
* Assuming confidence equals truth
* Using AI for critical decisions without review

---

# Safety Check

Remember:

* AI is a tool
* AI is not perfect
* Human oversight is essential
* Responsible use builds trust

---

# Real-World Challenge

Find one AI-generated answer and verify it independently.
