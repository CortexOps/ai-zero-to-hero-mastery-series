# AI Zero to Hero Glossary

This glossary explains important AI terms in beginner-friendly language.

---

# AI (Artificial Intelligence)

Technology that allows machines or software to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as writing, reasoning, summarizing, or problem-solving.

---

# Prompt

The instruction or question you give to an AI system.

### Example:

```text
Write a professional email requesting time off.
```

---

# LLM (Large Language Model)

A powerful AI model trained on massive amounts of text to understand and generate language.

Examples:

* ChatGPT
* Claude
* Llama
* Mistral

---

# Chatbot

An AI system designed to communicate through conversation.

---

# Hallucination

When AI produces incorrect or fabricated information that sounds believable.

---

# Verification

The process of checking AI output for accuracy.

---

# Public AI

Cloud-based AI tools accessible online.

Examples:

* ChatGPT
* Gemini
* Claude

---

# Local AI

AI models run on personal hardware for greater privacy and control.

Examples:

* Ollama
* Open WebUI
* LM Studio

---

# Automation

Using AI or software to reduce repetitive tasks.

---

# Prompt Engineering

The skill of designing prompts that produce better AI responses.

---

# Context

Background information provided to AI to improve output quality.

---

# Token

A small piece of text processed by AI systems.

---

# Fine-Tuning

Training or customizing AI models for specific tasks.

---

# Open Source AI

AI tools or models with publicly available code or weights.

---

# Workflow

A repeatable process that uses AI to complete tasks efficiently.

---

# Ethical AI

Using AI responsibly, safely, fairly, and transparently.

---

# Bias

Systematic unfairness or skewed behavior in AI outputs.

---

# Human Oversight

The requirement that humans remain responsible for final decisions.

---

# AI Agent

A system that can take multiple actions, make decisions, or automate processes.

---

# No-Code

Tools that allow automation or software building without programming.

---

# API (Application Programming Interface)

A way software systems communicate with one another.

---

# RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

A system where AI uses external documents or databases to improve answers.

---

# Inference

The process of AI generating outputs from prompts.

---

# Model

The trained AI system itself.

---

# Privacy

Protecting sensitive personal or organizational data.

---

# Governance

Rules, policies, and standards for responsible AI use.

---

# Deployment

The process of implementing AI tools in practical environments.

---

# Scaling

Expanding AI use across larger workflows or organizations.

---

# Final Advice

You do not need to memorize every term immediately.

Learn through use.
Practice consistently.
Return to this glossary whenever needed.
